KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY
================================================================================

QUESTION: Why are Tesla's EV sales and prices declining despite 30% market
          growth projected for 2025?

ANSWER: Market maturation + competitive disruption

TESLA Q1-2024 PERFORMANCE:
  • Sales: 140,187 units (-13.3% YoY) ❌
  • Market Share (USA): 51.3% (down from 61.7% Q1-23) ❌
  • Operating Margin: 5.5% (down from 10.9%) ❌
  • Free Cash Flow: -$2.5B ❌

MARKET CONTEXT:
  • Global BEV Growth: +59% (2021-22), 4.6M → 7.3M units ✓
  • USA BEV Growth: +70% (2021-22), 470k → 800k units ✓
  • Global CAGR 2010-22: ~60% annually ✓

FOUR HYPOTHESES - ALL VALIDATED:

1. GROWING COMPETITION ✅ CRITICAL
   Evidence: Ford +86%, Mercedes +66%, BMW +62%, Rivian +58.8% (Q1-24)
   Impact: Every competitor outpacing Tesla despite 51% market share

2. PRICE WARS & MARGIN COMPRESSION ✅ HIGH
   Evidence: 3 price cuts in 12 months; margins halved
   Impact: Operating margin down to 5.5%; negative FCF

3. INVENTORY BUILDUP & DEMAND WEAKNESS ✅ HIGH
   Evidence: $2.7B inventory increase Q1; negative FCF
   Impact: Market saturation signals in USA

4. CHARGING INFRASTRUCTURE ✅ MEDIUM
   Evidence: 51k stations in USA; Great Plains underserved
   Impact: Biden $7.5B investment levels playing field (reduces Tesla moat)
